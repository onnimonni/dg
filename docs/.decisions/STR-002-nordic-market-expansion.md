# Nordic Market Expansion

## Introduction

Following our successful French market entry, the Nordic region presents unique opportunities for Pied Piper's compression technology. Sweden, Norway, Denmark, and Finland represent a $2.3B addressable market with high technology adoption rates and a strong preference for data privacy. The Nordics are also a gateway to broader European B2B sales.

## Goals

1. Enter Sweden as our first Nordic market by Q3 2024
2. Achieve $200K ARR from Nordic customers by end of 2024
3. Establish banking relationships for SEK and EUR transactions
4. Sign partnership with at least one major Nordic enterprise (Spotify, Klarna, or similar)

## Tenets

1. **Privacy as product** - Nordic customers expect the highest privacy standards. Our compression maintains data integrity.
2. **English-first, local support** - Business operations in English, but customer support must be local.
3. **Developer community focus** - Nordic tech scene is developer-led. Win developers first.
4. **Sustainable growth** - Nordic business culture values long-term relationships over quick wins.

## State of the Business

### Current Metrics
- France ARR: $350K (on track)
- European pipeline: $1.2M
- Support tickets from EU: 45% resolved same-day
- Weissman Score: 5.2 (industry-leading compression)

### Market Context
- Sweden: Largest Nordic tech ecosystem (Spotify, Klarna, King)
- Norway: Strong oil/gas data processing needs
- Finland: Mobile gaming industry with massive data requirements
- Denmark: Enterprise SaaS hub for Scandinavia

### Competitive Position
- Strong: Best-in-class compression, proven EU compliance from France
- Weak: No Nordic presence, brand unknown in region
- Opportunity: Local competitors focus on storage, not compression

## Strategic Priorities

### Priority 1: Sweden Entry via Stockholm
Stockholm concentrates 70% of Nordic tech companies. Start here with a single sales rep and partnership with a local tech accelerator like SUP46 or STING.

### Priority 2: Banking Infrastructure
Traditional banks (Nordea, SEB) vs modern fintech (Revolut, Wise). Need SEK accounts for local invoicing. Decision required on primary banking partner.

### Priority 3: Developer Relations
Sponsor Nordic.js, participate in Stockholm Tech Meetups, launch Nordic-specific API documentation. The developer community drives enterprise adoption.

## FAQ

**Q: Why Sweden first and not Norway?**
A: Sweden has 2x the tech company density. Norway expansion can follow in 2025, leveraging Stockholm presence.

**Q: Can we share resources with French operations?**
A: Partially. Legal and compliance can be shared. Sales and support must be local.

**Q: What about the Finnish market?**
A: Finland is promising (mobile gaming, Nokia heritage) but smaller. Include in Phase 2 with Norway.

**Q: How does this relate to the Hooli threat?**
A: Hooli has no Nordic presence. Establishing here first creates a defensible position for when they eventually expand.
